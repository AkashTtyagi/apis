Attendance Rules: Possible & Feasible 

General Attendance Rules:
Employees shall observe the following rules while marking and managing attendance.

a)Attendance shall be marked only through bio-metric face reader, attendance machines installed at all offices/locations. There is a provision of marking attendance through the mobile application for only employees who are working out of office or on field. Attendance shall be marked at least twice; once at the time of entry and the other at the time of exiting the premises. The marked attendance will be called as “Punch” in the system. For completing attendance, there shall be both entry and exit punches, else the attendance shall be incomplete and employee marked absent. Possible & Feasible 

b)Employees, going out of premises during working hours for any emergencies shall obtain the permission from the supervisors and shall ensure they mark exit and entry in the attendance system. Possible & Feasible 

c)In case where the attendance marks/punches in the systems are missed out, the attendance shall be regularized in the system only after the same is approved by the Manager. Possible & Feasible 

d)A grace of 15 minutes at the beginning of the shift shall be given. For example, if the shift beginning time is 10 AM, the grace period shall end at 10.15 AM. If the attendance is marked, after the grace period, it will be treated as “Late” coming. Employees shall leave the workplace only at the end of shift timing. In case the attendance is marked earlier than the shift close timings, it will be treated as “Early” punch.  Every sixth “Late”  or “ Early “ Punches in a month in the system, will attract automatic deduction of half-day “Leave” from any of the available leave to the credit of employee. If there is no Leave Balance, equivalent amount of salary shall be deducted. Possible & Feasible 

e)Employees not completing 8 hours of duty on any assigned shift, will also be considered as half day leave. Possible & Feasible 


f)Employees after the attendance entry is registered, shall be required to complete 4 hours 30 minutes between entry and exit punches to be eligible for half-day attendance. Possible & Feasible 


Leave Benefits: Possible & Feasible 

In compliance with the statutory framework and the needs of the employee, following are the Leave Benefits are provided to employees.

a)Casual Leave (CL): Casual Leave shall be credited to the account of the employee at the beginning of the calendar year or on the date of joining on a pro rata basis. CL can be availed only for half day or one day and fractions of that shall not be allowed. 50% of the balance CL at the year-end shall be encashed, subject to maximum of six days.  CL cannot be carried forward. CL not availed shall lapse. CL cannot be availed more than 5 days at a time. Possible & Feasible 

b)Annual Leave (AL): Annual leave entitlement shall be credited to the account of the employee at the end of each calendar year. In case of new employees, AL shall be credited proportionately on completion of one year of service based on the date of joining. AL can be carried forward to the succeeding year with a limit of 30 days, the balance AL not availed shall be encashed. AL can be availed for a minimum period of one full day. Possible & Feasible 


c)Sick Leave (SL): Sick Leave shall be credited to leave account at the beginning of the year or on the date of joining on a pro rata basis. SL can be availed only on medical ground. Certificate from a registered medical practitioner (Allopathic, Homeopathic & Ayurvedic doctors only) shall be required, if numbers of SL exceeds three days in a row. Holidays & Weekly Off days coming in between SL shall not be treated as Sick Leave while calculating SL. SL shall be minimum half day. No fraction beyond this shall be permitted.  SL can be carried over to the succeeding year with the permissible limit of 90 days and the balance shall be encashed. Possible & Feasible

2. INTEREST FREE LOAN (STAFF LOAN): Possible & Feasible 

All confirmed employees who have completed 2 years’ service shall be eligible for staff loans from the company for meeting any of the following expenses.

a.Marriage of self or dependent family members.
b.Purchase of Vehicle.
c.Purchase of household durables.
d.Modification/renovation of House.
e.Purchase/construction of House
f.Medical treatment of self and dependents.
g.Education expenses of self and family members.

The loan shall carry ‘NIL’ interest and shall be repaid not more than 36 monthly installments.

Only one loan at a time shall be granted.  Employees, who have repaid 80 % of their loans, shall be permitted to foreclose the remaining loan and avail fresh loan. However a fresh loan can be availed only after completing 12 months from the date of sanctioning the previous loan.

All loans shall be subject to approval by the respective HODs.  Management however, reserves the right to deny loan facility to an employee without assigning any reasons.
In circumstances where, the management is convinced that the employee is put to hardship beyond his normal capacity, management may, at sole discretion grant a second loan within the overall eligibility. Raise requirement for this..

Loan shall be recovered in equal monthly installments from the employee’s salary falling due immediately after availing the loan.

4. OVERSEAS TRAVEL POLICY (Need to connect for clarity on current working process.)
This policy provides guidelines and establishes procedures and entitlement for travel, boarding and lodging for Employees, while traveling out of the country on official work.

Scope:
This policy is applicable to Employees of the company at all locations inclusive of corporate office, Parks and Resort who required to travel overseas for official purposes. 

Enforcement:
The Company will pay for all reasonable and necessary expenses while traveling on authorized Company business that are in compliance with the guidelines of the Overseas Travel Policy. The Company assumes no obligation to reimburse employees for expenses that are not in compliance with this policy.
Exceptions to Policy:
Exceptions, deviations or reimbursements for expenses that are not in compliance with this policy require the prior written approval of the Joint Managing Director / Executive Director.

Travel Authorization:
All foreign travel shall require prior approval in the “Overseas Travel Authorization Form”(OTAF). The same should authorized by not less than Executive Director. In case of changes in the tour duration the employee shall take approval for the same from the Department Head which shall be approved by not less than Executive Director.

Ticket Booking: 
Corporate Purchase Department shall arrange to book tickets after obtaining minimum 3 quotations and evaluating the same.  The request for ticket booking has to be accompanied by the OTAF.

Travel Advance: 
The Travel Advance shall be 150% of the Daily Allowances and calculated for the number of days. The same shall be collected by the employee before proceeding on to the travel from the Accounts Department at the location. This is made against the OTAF.

Mode of Travel:
Employees shall travel only by excursion or economy fare. Whereas, Directors are eligible for business class travel if the journey time exceeds 4 hours.  

Allowances & Reimbursement: 
Employees on business travel will be entitled for Daily Allowance & reimbursements under the heads mentioned below.

Daily Allowances: 
Employees who are on foreign travel are eligible for daily allowance as mentioned below which will be paid without any bills. The employees are not eligible to claim any other expense. No other Allowance will be paid in India while they are on foreign travel. The allowances are denominated in Dollar currency. However, Depending upon the country of visit, local currency equivalent to dollar currency as eligible will be issued. Travel bills should be settled immediately after return and the balance foreign currency if any should be returned to Accounts Department. All air travel should be supported with original boarding pass issued by the Airlines. E- Tickets will not be treated as proof for travel performed.
